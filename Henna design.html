                        Indian henna design, also known as mehndi, is a traditional form of 
                        body art that originated in ancient India. It involves the application
                        of a paste made from henna leaves to create intricate patterns on the
                        skin. Henna, or Lawsonia inermis, is a flowering plant whose leaves 
                        contain a natural dye that leaves a reddish-brown stain when applied 
                        to the skin.
